Viet
House

Viet House is a grand neoclassical residence in Ho Chi Minh City, designed to reflect a legacy of elegance and sophistication through its intricate architectural details. The material strategy focuses on tactile contrast within a restrained palette. Painted wall moldings in warm whites are paired with natural wood veneers and herringbone flooring to introduce depth and warmth. Stone surfaces—particularly marble in the kitchen and flooring—provide durability and visual weight, complemented by brushed metal accents in lighting and hardware. Upholstered elements in linen and leather soften the overall composition, ensuring the interiors feel livable rather than overly formal. Every material is selected not just for aesthetics, but for longevity and ease of maintenance within a compact urban footprint.
